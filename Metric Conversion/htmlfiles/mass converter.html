Mass

Mass is a fundamental property of matter, representing the amount of substance in an object. It is a measure of the total number of atoms, molecules, and particles in a body, and is commonly expressed in units such as kilograms or grams. Unlike weight, which depends on the gravitational force acting on an object, mass remains constant regardless of its location in the universe. This property plays a crucial role in various scientific disciplines, including physics, astronomy, and engineering. In physics, mass is a key factor in determining an object's inertia, which is its resistance to changes in motion. In astronomy, mass governs the gravitational interactions between celestial bodies, shaping the dynamics of galaxies, stars, and planets.
