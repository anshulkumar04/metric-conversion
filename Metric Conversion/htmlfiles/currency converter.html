Currency

Currency plays a fundamental role in the global economy, serving as a medium of exchange, a unit of account, and a store of value. It represents the value of goods and services in an economy and facilitates trade and economic transactions. Various countries have their own currencies, each with its unique symbols and denominations. The value of a currency can fluctuate based on economic factors, geopolitical events, and market sentiment. Central banks and governments often regulate and issue currency to maintain stability and promote economic growth. In the modern digital age, electronic transactions and cryptocurrencies have also gained prominence, reshaping the way we perceive and use currency in our everyday lives.
