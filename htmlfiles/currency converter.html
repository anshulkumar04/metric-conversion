Currency

Currency: The invention and evolution of currency are complex processes that have occurred over thousands of years. The concept of currency evolved from barter systems, with various items used as early forms of money, such as shells, livestock, and precious metals. Modern currency systems, like the use of paper money and coins, have diverse origins across different regions and time periods. The specific individuals who invented currency are unknown, but it developed in response to the needs of growing economies and trade. The first modern banknotes, for example, were introduced in Europe during the 17th century, and the concept of fiat money, not backed by physical assets like gold, developed over time and continues to evolve today.

Who: The concept of currency and coins has a long history that evolved across many civilizations. Modern currency, such as the United States Dollar, was developed by the U.S. government.

How: The U.S. Dollar was established by the U.S. Congress as the official currency of the United States and was initially based on the Spanish dollar, which was widely used in trade.
